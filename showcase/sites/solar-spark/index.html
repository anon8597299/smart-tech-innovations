💰 Government rebates available in Orange NSW

Solar buyers in Orange and Central West NSW have access to significant government incentives. We handle all the paperwork — you simply receive a lower invoice.

Federal
Small-Scale Technology Certificates (STCs)

The federal government's main solar rebate. Orange falls in STC Zone 3. A typical 10kW system currently attracts approximately $3,300 in STCs — deducted from your invoice upfront. We assign the STCs on your behalf so you never have to deal with the process.

NSW
NSW Feed-In Tariff & VPP Programs

Essential Energy pays a feed-in tariff for excess solar exported to the grid. Rates vary by retailer — currently 4–10c/kWh depending on your plan. SunVolt can advise on the best retailer for your usage profile and help you join Virtual Power Plant programs for additional income.

Finance
Interest-Free Solar Finance

Through our finance partners, eligible Orange homeowners can access 0% interest finance for up to 5 years. This means most systems are cash-flow positive from month one — your monthly repayment is less than your old power bill. Subject to credit approval.

Common Questions

Frequently asked questions

Straight answers from the SunVolt team — no sales spin.

How long does a solar installation take in Orange? ⌄
For a standard residential system (6.6kW–13.2kW), the physical installation takes one day. Our crew typically arrives at 7:30am and is packed up by late afternoon. We arrange the Essential Energy meter change and grid connection paperwork in parallel — most systems are approved and generating within 5–7 business days of the install date. Larger commercial systems or battery storage jobs may take 1–2 days.
What government rebates are available and how much are they worth? ⌄
The main rebate is the federal STC (Small-Scale Technology Certificate) scheme. Orange falls in STC Zone 3. As a rough guide: a 6.6kW system attracts approximately $2,200 in STCs, a 10kW system around $3,300, and a 13.2kW system around $4,300. These amounts are deducted directly from your invoice — you don't pay them and wait for a rebate. The STC scheme is set to reduce annually until 2030, so the rebate is higher if you install sooner. We also advise on NSW feed-in tariffs and any current state government battery incentive programs.
What happens to my solar during a blackout? ⌄
A standard grid-connect solar system (no battery) will shut down automatically during a blackout — this is a safety requirement to protect Essential Energy workers. If you have a compatible battery system (such as a Tesla Powerwall 3 or Sungrow SBH), the battery can island your home during a blackout, keeping lights and essential appliances running. This is a common question from families on rural properties around Orange, Molong, and Cargo where outages do occur. We can recommend the right battery for your backup needs during the assessment.
How much can I realistically save on my electricity bill? ⌄
For a typical Orange family spending around $400/month on electricity, a well-sized 10kW north-facing system typically reduces bills by 65–75%. Many of our customers' bills drop to near zero or go into credit in the warmer months. Savings depend on your orientation, shading, household usage profile, and how much power you use during daylight hours. Use our calculator above for a personalised estimate, then book a free on-site assessment for a precise figure. Our average customer across Central West NSW saves between $2,000 and $3,500 per year.
Is SunVolt Solar CEC accredited? Do you use subcontractors? ⌄
Yes — SunVolt Solar is fully CEC (Clean Energy Council) accredited. This means our designers and installers meet the highest industry standards and you remain eligible for STC rebates. We do not use subcontractors. Every assessment, design, and installation is completed by our own employed and accredited team based in Orange. This is important to us — it's how we maintain our 4.9-star rating and 10-year workmanship warranty. If there's ever an issue, you call us directly and we come back. Simple as that.
Do you service areas outside Orange? ⌄
Yes — we regularly install across the Central West. Our standard service area includes Bathurst, Dubbo, Parkes, Cowra, Forbes, Molong, Blayney, Canowindra, Grenfell, Eugowra, and surrounding areas. For properties beyond our usual range, contact us to check availability — we travel for larger commercial and farm jobs. We're familiar with Essential Energy's requirements for the entire Central West distribution area.
