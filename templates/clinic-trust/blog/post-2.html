Choosing a Doctor

How to Find the Right GP in {{SUBURB}}: A Practical Guide

By {{BUSINESS_NAME}}  ·  {{SUBURB}}, {{STATE}}  ·  Patient Resources

Finding a GP in {{SUBURB}} is easy — finding the right GP takes a little more thought. A good general practitioner is one of the most important ongoing relationships in your healthcare journey. Unlike a specialist you see once or twice, your GP is meant to know your history, your risk factors, your family circumstances, and your health goals. That kind of knowledge doesn't happen in a single appointment; it builds over years. This guide will help you find someone worth building that relationship with.

Start with Practical Logistics

Before you assess clinical quality, make sure the practical fundamentals work for your life. A great GP you can never actually get in to see is less useful than a solid GP with an appointment in three days.

  • Location: Can you get there from home or work without significant effort? Particularly important for routine check-ups or when you're unwell.
  • Hours: Does the clinic offer appointments before 9am or after 5pm? What about Saturdays? Extended hours matter if you work standard business hours.
  • Booking system: Can you book online? Is there a phone wait time? How far out are routine appointments typically available?
  • Bulk billing: Does the practice bulk bill, and if so, for which patients? (See our separate guide on bulk billing for more detail.)

What to Look for in a GP

Once the logistics work, look at clinical fit. The right GP for you depends on your stage of life and health needs:

  • Communication style: Do they explain things clearly without being condescending? Do they make space for your questions?
  • Thoroughness: Do they take a history rather than just treating the presenting symptom? Do they order appropriate investigations?
  • Interest in preventive care: Do they remind you about health checks, screenings, and vaccinations appropriate for your age?
  • Specialist referral network: A well-connected GP in {{SUBURB}} will be able to direct you to trusted local specialists when needed.
  • Comfort discussing sensitive topics: Mental health, sexual health, substance use — these require a GP who doesn't dismiss or minimise.

Questions Worth Asking Before You Commit

You are entitled to ask questions before establishing a patient relationship with a clinic. A few worth raising:

  • Are you taking new patients?
  • Will I generally see the same GP at each visit?
  • What happens when my regular GP is unavailable?
  • Do you have GPs with particular interest areas relevant to me (women's health, skin, mental health, chronic disease management)?
  • What is the typical wait time for a routine appointment?
  • Do you have an after-hours service or can you direct me to one?

The Importance of Continuity of Care

Research consistently shows that patients who see the same GP regularly have better health outcomes, fewer hospital admissions, and report greater satisfaction with their care. This happens because your GP builds a longitudinal picture of your health that no walk-in clinic or urgent care service can replicate.

Continuity also means your GP will notice gradual changes that you might not — a slow rise in blood pressure over years, a shift in mood across multiple appointments, or a pattern in your pathology results that warrants investigation. These things require a history, and a history requires a consistent relationship.

After Your First Appointment

A first appointment is as much about assessment as treatment. Pay attention to how you felt after: Did you feel heard? Were your questions answered? Did the GP explain their reasoning? Were they respectful of your time? It's reasonable to try a different doctor within the same practice, or a different clinic entirely, if the first appointment didn't feel right. Most people know quickly whether a GP is a good fit.
