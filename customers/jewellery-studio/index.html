Before You Arrive

What you need to know

We work with real metals, open flame and sharp tools. Please read this before your class — it keeps everyone safe and sessions running smoothly.

Enclosed Shoes — No Exceptions Metal filings, offcuts and hot pieces can fall. No thongs, sandals, heels or open-toe shoes. Sneakers or work boots are perfect.
Wear Clothes You Don't Mind Marking You'll work with solder flux, polishing compounds and metal dust. Natural fibres are better than synthetics near a torch.
Tie Back Long Hair Soldering involves open flame. Long hair must be fully tied back. Loose, baggy sleeves should be rolled up or avoided.
Remove Your Jewellery Rings, bracelets and dangling jewellery interfere with tools and can be a hazard near flame. Leave them in your bag for the session.
Eat Beforehand Sessions run 2–4 hours and you'll be standing and focused the whole time. A proper meal or solid snack before you arrive makes a big difference.
Common Sense in the Studio Listen to your instructor. Ask if you're unsure. Don't rush steps that need care — we'd rather slow down than have an accident.
